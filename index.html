Featured Projects

Analysing Student’s Mental Health

Tools used: SQL

In this project, I analysed mental health data from international students at a Japanese university from Datacamp to understand the potential impact of studying abroad on mental health. Using SQL queries, I conducted an exploratory analysis, exploring key variables such as social connectedness, acculturative stress, and depression levels to uncover trends and patterns.


The study, conducted in 2018, revealed that international students face a higher risk of mental health difficulties compared to the general population. My analysis aimed to validate these findings and investigate additional factors, such as the length of stay, as a potential contributor to mental health challenges.


By querying and analysing the dataset, I identified correlations between cultural adaptation, social belonging, and depression levels.

Analysing International Debt Statistics

Tools used: SQL

In this project, I used SQL queries to analyse international debt data collected by the World Bank from Datacamp. Emphasis is placed on developing countries and their economic reliance on debt. The dataset included detailed information about debt amounts (USD) across various categories, providing a comprehensive view of how countries manage their financial obligations. The analysis sought to answer key questions such as:


  • How many distinct countries are represented in the database?
  • Which country has the highest total debt?
  • Which country has the lowest repayment amounts?

By querying the dataset, I uncovered insights into global debt patterns, highlighting which countries have the greatest debt burdens and exploring repayment behaviours.

Analysing Customer Churn for Databel Customers

In this project, I utilised Tableau to analyse customer churn data. Focused on understanding the reasons behind customer contract termination and identifying patterns to help reduce churn rates. The dataset provided insights into customer attributes, including demographics, subscription types, charges, and customer behaviour. The analysis covered key areas such as:

  • Identifying the primary reasons for customer churn.
  • Analysing churn rates across demographic groups (e.g., age, gender, and group contracts).
  • Investigating how contract length, payment methods, and monthly charges contribute to churn.
  • Exploring the impact of customer service calls and international plans on churn behaviour.

Using Tableau's visualisation capabilities, I developed interactive dashboards to present findings and identify trends with actionable insights.
